Bogl Explorer

* This is a prototype tool, and a work in progress...

This tool is meant to help structure your understanding of BoGL programs. If you are learning BoGL syntax, this tool can help you understand:
- what concepts follow from a given program
- or, how two programs in BoGL are related, and what order of concepts you need to learn to go from one program to the next.

  1. Enter a Known BoGL program in the top box.
  2. (optional) Enter a Goal BoGL program you want to know in the bottom box (ideally a little more advanced than the known program).
      3. If you don't add a goal program, all programs that can be built from the Known program will be shown.
  3. Click Search to see the next programs you can learn.
  4. Next programs you can learn are listed below by name, concepts they introduce, and their content; see the following example:

    Program Name

    This program introduces the concepts: A,B,C

    ...program content that uses concepts A,B,C...
  5. Below the programs a graph shows the relationship between your Known and Goal programs. The top of the graph being your known program, and the bottom being your goal. The directed edges are followed to determine concepts and programs that show up in your search results.
      (hint: If the graph or your search results get too much, try working with programs that are simpler and have fewer differences, and gradually build up to what you want to see.)
